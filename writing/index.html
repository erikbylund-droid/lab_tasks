Sample answer (text)

Hi Katy,

I was so happy to get your email. I'm really excited about our camping trip with your family. Thanks for inviting me!

I've never tried rafting or rock climbing before. It sounds very scary! I'd like to do some activities that are less dangerous like hiking or swimming. Is that ok?

The idea of having big meals together in the evenings sounds fantastic. I absolutely love burgers and barbeque!

I have an idea for something special during the trip. Let's do a campfire at night with marshmallows and stories. What do you think?

I hope to hear from you soon!
Take care,
John

Why this is a strong answer

  • Content: It follows all four notes — thanks Katy, reacts to adventure sports with a polite “no, but…” and alternatives, shows enthusiasm for the food, and suggests something special (the campfire).
  • Tone: Informal and warm (“Hi Katy”, “Thanks for inviting me!”), right for a friend.
  • Organisation: Logical paragraphs, clear opening and closing, questions where appropriate.
  • Language: Natural B1 linking and vocabulary (“sounds fantastic”, “I'd like to”, “What do you think?”) with mostly accurate grammar. (The original exam sheet had “It sound”; “It sounds” is the standard form.)
  • Length: Fits the kind of length expected for a 100–140 word Part 1 email.

What we look for when we mark your email: In B1 Preliminary Writing Part 1, examiners assess content (you answer the task and cover every point the notes ask for), communicative achievement (the tone fits an informal email to a friend; the reader would get the message clearly), organisation (logical paragraphs, greetings and closings, cohesive devices), and language (range and control of grammar and vocabulary appropriate to B1).
