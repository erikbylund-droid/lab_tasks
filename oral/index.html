Oral Task 1 — Photo Description

B1 level · PET Part 2 style

Sample of an Oral Task
Task: Describe this photo fully. Include:
  • Place: Where is it? What kind of place?
  • People: Who can you see? What are they doing?
  • Actions: What is happening?
  • Atmosphere: How does it feel? (e.g. relaxed, busy)

Speak for about 1 minute.

Sample answer (model response)

In this picture I can see a beach on a sunny day. The place looks like a popular seaside resort because there is a lot of sand and the sea is in the background, with quite big waves.

There are several people in the photo. In the foreground I can see a family or a group of friends walking along the shore, and there are children as well. Some people seem to be enjoying the water or standing near the edge of the sea.

What is happening is that everyone is relaxing and spending time outdoors. Some are probably playing or chatting, and others are just taking a walk. It looks like a typical summer day at the beach.

The atmosphere is very calm and pleasant. It feels peaceful and happy — like a holiday — and the bright sky makes the scene look really inviting. Overall, it is a positive and relaxed situation.

Why this answer aims for the highest band

  • Task: It answers every part of the prompt — place, people, actions, and atmosphere — so the examiner can tick all the boxes for a full response.
  • Place: It says what kind of location it is (beach, seaside), adds detail (sand, sea, waves) and uses language of speculation where needed (“looks like a popular resort”).
  • People: It distinguishes foreground and background ideas, mentions different groups (family or friends, children) and avoids repeating only “some people.”
  • Actions: It describes what is going on (walking, enjoying the water, relaxing) and uses hedging (“seem to,” “probably”) appropriately for a photo you are interpreting.
  • Atmosphere: It names feelings (calm, peaceful, happy) and explains them with reference to the scene (bright sky, holiday feeling), ending with a clear summary (“positive and relaxed”).
  • Organisation: Each paragraph matches one main idea, with logical order and simple connectors (“in the foreground,” “overall”), which is easy to follow in a one-minute turn.
  • Language: It uses B1–B2 range (e.g. “in the background,” “typical,” “inviting”) and a mix of present tenses and natural phrases for description, with generally accurate grammar.
