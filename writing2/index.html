Writing Task 2 — PET Part 2 Story

B1 level · continuous writing (story option)

In the real B1 Preliminary Writing paper, Part 1 is always an email. In Part 2 you choose one task: often an article or a story. For the story, you must write about 100 words, and your answer must begin with the exact sentence you are given (same words, spelling, and punctuation). Examiners expect a short narrative with a clear beginning, middle, and end — usually with past tenses and simple linking (“then,” “suddenly,” “after that”). Very short answers (well under the target length) typically lose marks.

Sample story (model answer — different opening)

The exam might give any opening line; this model shows structure and length for a Part 2 story. Your task below uses the required opening When I opened the door…

Sample story (text)

As the bus stopped at my street, I realised I had left my keys on the kitchen table. I felt worried because my parents wouldn’t be home until late.

I rang the doorbell and waited, but nobody answered. Then I remembered our neighbour Mrs Cole had a spare key. While I was walking to her flat, my phone rang — it was my mum. She explained that Dad was already on his way.

A few minutes later he arrived with the keys and we laughed about my mistake. Next time I will always check my bag before leaving.

Why this fits PET Part 2

  • Opening: Uses the prompt as the starting point without changing it (in your task this will be “When I opened the door…”).
  • Plot: Problem → attempts to solve → resolution; stays connected to the situation.
  • Language: Past simple / continuous and basic linkers (“then,” “while,” “a few minutes later”).
  • Length: About 100 words — appropriate for Part 2.

What we look for: Content — the story follows from the opening and feels complete. Communicative achievement — neutral to informal narrative style, clear for the reader. Organisation — logical order and simple cohesive devices. Language — appropriate narrative tenses and B1 vocabulary, with reasonable accuracy. Check spelling and punctuation before you download.
