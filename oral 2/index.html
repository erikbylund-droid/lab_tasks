Oral Task 2 — Personal Long Turn

B1 level · PET Part 1 topics, extended to a one-minute turn

In the real B1 Preliminary Speaking test, Part 1 is a short interview: the examiner asks each candidate personal or factual questions for about two minutes in total. Part 2 is the “long turn”: you talk on your own for about one minute (often describing a photo). This practice task keeps the personal focus of Part 1 but asks you to speak continuously for about a minute, like the timing of a long turn — useful for fluency, organising ideas, and giving detail.

Sample long turn (model response)
Sample topic: Talk about a time you learnt something new.

Sample answer

I'd like to talk about when I learnt to cook a proper meal by myself — not just sandwiches, but something we could eat as a family. It was last winter, during the holidays, and I was at home with my mum in the kitchen.

At first I was nervous because I'm not naturally good at following recipes, but my mum explained everything step by step. We prepared a simple pasta with tomato sauce and vegetables. While I was chopping the onions my eyes watered a lot and we laughed about it — that helped me relax.

In the end the dish tasted much better than I expected, and everyone said it was good. I felt really proud because I learnt that if you concentrate and practise, you can improve quickly. I'd love to cook something more difficult next time, maybe a cake.

Why this works at B1

  • Task: It chooses one clear experience (“learning something new”), not three topics at once.
  • Detail: It answers implicit “wh-” questions: who (with mum), where (kitchen), when (last winter), what happened (recipe, feelings, result).
  • Organisation: Set-up → problem/feelings → what happened → ending and reflection; easy to follow for a one-minute turn.
  • Language: Past tenses and sequencers (“at first,” “while,” “in the end”), opinions and future wish — natural for narrative and reflection.
  • Fluency: Enough content to keep going; you can add one more sentence if you finish early.
Your task: Give a personal long turn on one of the following. Choose only one topic and stick to it.
  • an experience where you learnt something new, or
  • a trip or journey you remember, or
  • a challenge you faced and how you dealt with it.

Include:

  • What & when: Briefly say what happened and roughly when.
  • People / place: Who was there and where were you?
  • What happened: Say what you did step by step, or how events developed.
  • Thoughts: How you felt then, what you learnt, or what you might do next time.

Speak for about 1 minute. Aim to keep talking; if you run out of ideas, add reasons, examples, or a short conclusion.

What we listen for: relevance to your chosen topic; clear organisation of ideas; range and accuracy of grammar and vocabulary; natural pronunciation and fluency.
